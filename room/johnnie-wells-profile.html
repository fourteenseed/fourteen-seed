Digital project lead at Meor Studio in St Ives, a full-service creative agency delivering websites, platforms and digital experiences for hospitality, tourism, retail and public sector clients. Former three-term Mayor of St Ives Town Council. Here to move from using AI instinctively to applying it deliberately — in two very different rooms.

Born and raised in St Ives. Built a business here. Served the community here. Former competitive bodyboarder — UK representative at the 1993 and 1994 World Championships in Pipeline, Hawaii. That same place-rooted stubbornness shapes how he approaches problems.

What I'm exploring

Two parallel goals, running in tandem. Inside Meor Studio: how AI can sharpen discovery, improve delivery, and produce better client outcomes. Inside under-resourced local government: how civic bodies like St Ives Town Council can use smarter tools for better decisions, more efficient operations, and services that actually reflect what communities need.

Top five skills

  1. Digital delivery. 13+ years taking projects from discovery to launch across agency, consultancy and public sector. Things ship because he makes sure they do.
  2. Stakeholder management. Council chambers, corporate clients, community groups — knows how to align competing interests and keep things moving in complex environments.
  3. Civic leadership. Three terms as Mayor of St Ives, stepping down earlier this month. Understands how public bodies actually work: the politics, the constraints, the relationships that matter.
  4. Commercial range. Before Meor: Ford dealership GM, £5m Michelin sales territory, Cornwall Council change programmes. Operated across retail, corporate and public sector.
  5. Knowing a place deeply. Born and raised in St Ives, built a business here, serves the community here. That rootedness shapes every problem he approaches.

Where I'm weaker

A practitioner, not a technical builder. His relationship with AI has been intuitive rather than systematic. He's here to change that.

What's driving me

Two things in parallel. Meor is at an inflection point — AI is already changing how creative agencies work, and he wants to be the person in the studio who has thought seriously about it, not just kept pace. At the same time, six years in civic leadership showed him first-hand how much St Ives Town Council — and bodies like it — could benefit from smarter tools. He wants to build enough knowledge to propose the right things in both rooms, not just ask the right questions.

What I can bring to the cohort

  • A real public sector use case and an insider's understanding of how local government actually operates — useful for anyone building in civic, community or place-based spaces.
  • Over a decade of project delivery experience — scope, sequencing, delivery risk, keeping things real.
  • A grounded read. He'll say when something sounds good but won't survive contact with reality.

What I'd like from the cohort

  • Structured AI knowledge built in the right order, with people to think alongside.
  • Challenge on the St Ives Council ideas from people who'll spot what's missing.
  • The accountability of a room where everyone is doing the work.
