# Johnnie Wells

Hi, I'm Johnnie. I'm a founding member of **Meor Studio** in St Ives — a full-service creative agency where I lead digital project delivery across websites, platforms and digital experiences for hospitality, tourism, retail and public sector clients. I also recently stepped down after three consecutive terms as Mayor of St Ives, which has given me a clear-eyed view of what under-resourced civic organisations need — and what they're missing.

I'm in this cohort to close a gap — to move from someone who uses AI instinctively to someone who applies it deliberately. I'm here with two parallel goals: understanding how AI can genuinely transform the way Meor Studio works — sharper discovery, smarter delivery, better client outcomes — and exploring how it can serve St Ives Town Council and similar civic bodies: better decisions, more efficient operations, services that actually reflect what communities need.

## What I'm exploring

How AI can make a practical difference in two worlds: inside a creative agency — improving the way we scope, deliver and communicate — and inside under-resourced local government bodies, where capacity is tight and the stakes for real people are high.

## Top five skills

1. **Digital delivery.** 13+ years taking projects from discovery to launch across agency, consultancy and public sector. Things ship because I make sure they do.
2. **Stakeholder management.** Council chambers, corporate clients, community groups — I know how to align competing interests and keep things moving in complex environments.
3. **Civic leadership.** Three terms as Mayor of St Ives, stepping down earlier this month. I understand how public bodies actually work: the politics, the constraints, the relationships that matter.
4. **Commercial range.** Before Meor: Ford dealership GM, £5m Michelin sales territory, Cornwall Council change programmes. I've operated across retail, corporate and public sector.
5. **Knowing a place deeply.** Born and raised here, built a business here, serve the community here. Former competitive bodyboarder — UK representative at the '93 and '94 World Championships in Pipeline, Hawaii. That same place-rooted stubbornness shapes how I approach problems.

## Where I'm weaker

I'm a practitioner, not a technical builder. My relationship with AI has been intuitive rather than systematic, and I'm here to change that.

## What's driving me

Two things, running in parallel. Meor is at an inflection point — AI is already changing how creative agencies work, and I want to be the person in the studio who's thought seriously about it, not just keeping pace. At the same time, having spent six years in civic leadership I've seen first-hand how much St Ives Town Council — and bodies like it — could benefit from smarter tools. I want to build enough knowledge to propose the right things in both those rooms, not just ask the right questions.

## What I can bring to the cohort

- A real public sector use case and an insider's understanding of how local government actually operates — useful for anyone building in civic, community or place-based spaces.
- Over a decade of project delivery experience — scope, sequencing, delivery risk, keeping things real.
- A grounded read. I'll say when something sounds good but won't survive contact with reality.

## What I'd like from the cohort

- Structured AI knowledge built in the right order, with people to think alongside.
- Challenge on my St Ives Council ideas from people who'll spot what I'm missing.
- The accountability of a room where everyone is doing the work.
